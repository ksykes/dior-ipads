L’Officiel de la couture, 1949

© Les Éditions Jalou « L’Officiel 1949 »

Debutante Mavis Powell received this high-end American commercial copy of Dior’s Vénus as a surprise gift from her parents.

It was sold by Levy’s, who called it “A work of art.”

She only wore it once to her coming-out afternoon tea dance in Florida. Straps were added to make the design demure.

This is an authorized copy by the high-end American company Nanty (1920s-50s).

Nanty Frocks (1920s-50s) was a high-end American manufacturer that produced copies of Paris and London couture models, purchased by their buyer, Charles Rendigs. In the fall of 1949, Charles James was the company designer.
